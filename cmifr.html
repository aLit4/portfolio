CMIFR is a clinic center specialized in occupational medicine and work accidents. They are an industry leader due to their range of services, infrastructure, and innovation.

They continuously seek to update their services to current trends and develop new businesses units to focus on improving health in the workplace.

The Business Problem

CMIFR main problems were misinformation on their website, unavailable content on mobile, lack of visibility on other channels, including search engines. These issues didn't reflect the company's high-quality services resulting in an overload call center with repeated inquiries and lost business opportunities.

The Approach

I started this project by doing analysis and research.

Due to budget constraints, I couldn't do user interviews for research. Instead, I analyzed the reviews and comments made on every profile of the company to understand the user's pain points. I also examined the most recurrent inquiries registered in the call center to outline this information on the website.

Then, I tested the company website to analyze every usability problem, unclear content, misleading structure, and unoptimized code.

After I analyzed this information, I decided to implement a content-based strategy instead of a traditional pure marketing strategy. Although this approach seems slower at first, it has better results to create a more positive relationship with users and to position the brand as an expert.

The Process

For the strategy, I defined the goals for the project based on the business problems and some key indicators to measure progress.

I created user personas to differentiate the needs and goals of the users interacting with the CMIFR.

I divided this information into stages accordingly to the circumstances of every user touchpoint.

With these stages in mind, I developed essential information needed for achieving each stages goals.

I poured this content into a new corporate website, with a clear architecture, fixing all usability problems and with an experience designed for mobile. I also coded the website SEO-friendly. Also, I developed a company's blog to give useful information to the users and to post it on social media as a way to generate more traffic and interest to the website.

The Results

The users responded very well to the website and the content proposal we made over time. Clients learned in more depth the services of the CMIFR. This way, I was able to reduce pain point and improved the overall experience of interacting with CMIFR.

Measuring Success

In six months, the number of followers on social media and visitors on the website was duplicated organically. These actions generate new business opportunities for the clinic.

The project was successful in reaching our main goals. It will be important to keep updating content and run some usability testing to collect feedback and iterate on the strategy.
